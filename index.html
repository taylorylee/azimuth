What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files stored on your device by a website. They help websites remember your preferences, maintain sessions, and understand how visitors use the site. Some cookies are essential for basic functionality; others are used for analytics or personalization.

02
Cookies We Use

Strictly necessary cookies — required for the website to function. They enable core features such as page navigation, form submission, and secure login. No consent is required for these cookies under applicable law.

Analytics cookies — help us understand how visitors interact with our site (pages visited, time spent, errors encountered). We use anonymized analytics and do not share this data with third-party advertisers. Consent is requested before placing these cookies for users in applicable jurisdictions (EU, UK).

Preference cookies — remember your settings and choices (theme, language, display preferences) so you do not need to re-enter them each visit. Consent is requested before placing these cookies where required by law.

We do not use advertising, tracking, or profiling cookies. We do not permit third-party ad networks to place cookies through our services.

Your Consent & Choices

For users in the European Union, United Kingdom, and other jurisdictions with cookie consent requirements, we present a consent banner on first visit. You may accept, reject, or configure non-essential cookies at any time via our cookie preferences link in the site footer.

You may also configure your browser to block or delete cookies at any time. Note that disabling certain cookies may affect functionality of our web services:

  • Chrome: Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Manage Website Data
  • Firefox: Options → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
  • Edge: Settings → Cookies and site permissions

App Store Compliance
Reference guide for App Store submission Guidelines 3.1.1 / 5.1 / EULA requirements
This page summarizes Azimuth Studio's compliance posture for App Store review. It is not a user-facing legal document but a reference for Apple reviewers and internal use.
01
Required links for App Store listing

Apple requires the following URLs to be specified in App Store Connect:

  • Privacy Policy URL — must be publicly accessible, specific to the app, and current. Required for all apps.
  • Terms of Use / EULA URL — required if your app has in-app purchases or custom license terms. Apple's standard EULA is used by default unless a custom one is provided.
  • Support URL — a page where users can get help. Required for all apps.
02
Data use disclosures (App Store Connect)

Under Apple's App Privacy nutrition label requirements, you must disclose all data collected:

  • Location — Precise or coarse location; purpose: app functionality (mapping). User permission required. Linked to user: No.
  • Identifiers — Device ID used for crash reporting and analytics. Linked to user: No.
  • Usage data — Product interaction data for analytics. Linked to user: No.
  • Diagnostics — Crash data. Linked to user: No.
  • Contact info — Email address if account is created. Linked to user: Yes.

Data is not used for third-party advertising and is not sold. Indicate "Data Not Linked to You" for all categories except contact info.

03
Age rating & content

Apps must be accurately rated. Civic mapping and design research tools with no user-generated social content typically rate 4+ or 9+. If the app includes user-generated content or community features (e.g., shared map annotations), rate 12+ and enable content moderation or reporting mechanisms as required by Guideline 1.2.

04
In-app purchases

If Azimuth Studio apps include paid features, subscriptions, or premium content, all transactions must use Apple's in-app purchase system (Guideline 3.1.1). Directing users to external payment systems for digital goods is prohibited. Physical goods and services rendered outside the app are exempt.

05
Key App Store Review guidelines
  • Guideline 1.1 — No objectionable content
  • Guideline 2.1 — App completeness: no placeholder content or broken links at submission
  • Guideline 3.1.1 — In-app purchase requirements
  • Guideline 4.0 — Design standards: apps must function as described, not just wrap a website
  • Guideline 5.1.1 — Privacy policy required; must cover all data types collected
  • Guideline 5.1.2 — Permission request strings must clearly explain purpose (e.g., "Location is used to show community spaces near you")
06
Submission checklist
  • Privacy Policy URL added to App Store Connect and live on the web
  • EULA URL added if custom license terms are used
  • Support URL live and responsive
  • App Privacy nutrition labels completed for all data types
  • All permission usage description strings (NSLocationWhenInUseUsageDescription, etc.) populated in Info.plist
  • Age rating questionnaire completed accurately
  • All in-app purchases configured through App Store Connect
  • No references to competing platforms in app text or metadata
  • App tested on latest iOS version and current hardware
  • Screenshot assets and preview video comply with App Store metadata guidelines
